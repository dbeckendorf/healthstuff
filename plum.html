The Health Benefits of

Plums

Plums are incredibly healthy for us. This should come as no surprise, because cherries are incredibly healthy, too, and cherries are technically a species of plum. The species of plum we buy most in the store is Prunus domestica, while cherries are ususally Prunus avium. Not surprisingly, they have quite a few benefits in common, but one is merely MORE effective than the other.

It is also noteworthy that prunes are merely dehydrated plums, just like raisins are dehydrated grapes. Any health benefit that comes from plums will also come from prunes.

Constipation

Plums have been shown to be more effective at reducing constipation that psyllium, which is the main active ingredient in Metamucil. This is due partly to the fiber content of plums, and partly due to a chemical called sorbitol, which is a sugar alcohol with laxative effects. That being said, too many plums cause diarrhea. For this reason it is important to limit prune intake to 1/4 to 1/2 a cup per day. If you are drinking prune juice instead of eating prunes, limit the intake to 4 to 8 ounces per day.

Diabetes

A study in the journal Biomedical Research has shown that plums aid in reducing blood sugar and trigyceride leels.They do this in two ways. The first is that they are really high in fiber. Fiber slows the rate at which the body absorbs carbs (sugar), making the blood sugar level rise slowly instead of spike. The second is that they increased levels of adinopectin, a hormone that plays a role in blood sugar levels. Another study published in The British Medical Journalstates that the flavonoids present in plums exert protective effects against insulin resistance and help lower the risk of type-2 diabetes.
